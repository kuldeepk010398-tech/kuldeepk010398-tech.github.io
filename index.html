About

As a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Central Florida, my research focuses on advancing the field of microphysiological systems (MPS) and organ-on-chip systems to replicate inter-organ metabolic interactions for translational research. With over two years of experience, I have led multidisciplinary projects, including the development of biomimetic platforms that model heart, liver, and skeletal muscle systems, as well as preclinical Beagle-based physiological systems for pharmacokinetics and toxicology studies. My expertise in nanotechnology, microphysiological systems, and analytical chemistry supports innovative solutions for complex biomedical challenges.

Previously, I served as Head of Research and Development at Flavin Labs, where I directed the establishment of cutting-edge nanofertilizer laboratories and spearheaded impactful projects in nutraceuticals and poultry management. My strategic approach enabled the successful execution of innovative research initiatives. Passionate about advancing science, I am committed to fostering collaboration and delivering translational solutions that address critical needs in biotechnology, agriculture, and biomedical engineering.
